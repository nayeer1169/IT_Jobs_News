## Global IT Job Situation

Global technology hiring remains narrow. The clearest demand is in AI engineering, applied AI product work, cloud infrastructure, cybersecurity, data engineering, platform engineering, enterprise software, and customer-facing technical roles. Companies are still hiring for roles tied to revenue, automation, security, and infrastructure reliability.

The weak areas remain routine support, manual QA without automation, duplicated internal tooling, back-office operations, and non-critical coordination roles. AI is an important factor, but it is not the only reason for layoffs. Tom's Hardware, citing Challenger data, reported that AI ranked behind broader market conditions and restructuring as a stated reason for cuts in 2026.

## India IT Job Situation

India's IT market is showing "jobless growth": revenue is rising faster than hiring. Economic Times reported that the six largest IT services firms grew combined revenue from $95.9 billion in FY23 to $103.1 billion in FY26 while headcount remained broadly steady at about 1.9 million. Active technology job openings fell to about 93,000 in June 2026 from 112,000 a year earlier.

For freshers, this means competition is high. The market is not closed, but employers are selecting for job-ready skills, internships, project work, communication, and ability to work with AI/cloud/data tools. Mass hiring is weaker than earlier cycles, so freshers should apply across official company portals, startups, GCCs, internships, apprenticeships, QA/testing, support engineering, and junior developer roles.
