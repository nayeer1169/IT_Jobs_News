## Global Situation

The tech industry is witnessing a structural shift. The trend of high-volume pandemic hiring has completely reversed, and 2026 is defined by organizations restructuring to fund intensive artificial intelligence operations. Rather than simple cost-cutting, companies are redirecting capital to computational resources and AI infrastructure, leading to a flatter organizational layout and reductions in legacy development and middle-management roles.

**Roles at higher risk:**
- Middle management and operational coordination layers
- Non-automated QA testing and junior programming roles
- Customer support and operations teams impacted by AI-driven automation
- Legacy systems support and administrative roles

**Roles less exposed:**
- AI researchers, LLM fine-tuning specialists, and ML engineers
- Cloud architects, DevSecOps specialists, and Site Reliability Engineers (SREs)
- Cybersecurity and defensive network security operations
- Core data engineering (ETL pipeline development and maintenance)

## India Situation

The hiring market in major Indian IT hubs remains subdued due to conservative spending by US clients and global macroeconomic pressures. Startups and IT service providers are prioritizing operational efficiency, with many developers looking to cross-skill in cloud computing, data pipelines, and AI integrations to mitigate job security risks.
