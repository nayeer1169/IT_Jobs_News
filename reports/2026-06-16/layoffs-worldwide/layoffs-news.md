## Global Situation

The tech industry's layoffs are shifting. While 2024–2025 focused on broad post-pandemic cost-cutting, 2026 is defined by structural reorganization. A Tech Times study showed that cuts have hit 1,115 workers daily, often justified by AI integrations, though critics note these cuts do not always yield immediate financial returns. Companies like Robinhood are actively stripping away middle management to create flatter, faster-moving hierarchies.

**Roles at higher risk:**
- Middle management and operational coordination layers
- Manual testing and QA roles that do not employ automated frameworks
- Administrative support and customer service
- Offshore engineering teams in groups refocusing on domestic centralization (e.g., Opendoor India)

**Roles less exposed:**
- Machine Learning engineers and NLP researchers
- Cloud architects, SREs, and Platform engineering teams
- Core data engineers maintaining ETL pipelines
- Security engineering and defensive operations specialists

## India Situation

Indian developers face a dual challenge: the slowdown in traditional IT service headcount and the centralization trends of U.S. parent companies (such as Opendoor shutting down its India engineering division). Headcount growth in major IT hubs remains muted, but specialized roles in product engineering and domestic AI startups (like Sarvam AI) are showing resilience. Developers are increasingly focused on cross-skilling into cloud computing and AI tooling to secure new roles.
