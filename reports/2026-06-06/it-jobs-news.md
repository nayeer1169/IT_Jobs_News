## Global IT Job Situation

Global IT hiring is active only in selected areas. AI spending is increasing, but that does not automatically create broad hiring. Many companies are funding AI infrastructure and automation by reducing routine or overlapping jobs.

The strongest demand areas today are:

- AI engineering and applied AI product work
- cloud infrastructure and DevOps/platform engineering
- cybersecurity
- data engineering and analytics
- enterprise software implementation
- customer-facing technical support and solution roles

The weakest areas are:

- routine support roles
- manual QA without automation skills
- duplicated internal tooling roles
- back-office operations
- non-critical project coordination roles

The most important caveat: layoff numbers differ by source because trackers count different scopes. Layoffs.fyi is a public tech layoff tracker. Challenger-style data, as reported by Tom's Hardware, measures announced cuts in the US tech sector. These figures should not be added together.

## India IT Job Situation

India's large IT services firms are still cautious. Moneycontrol reported that TCS, Infosys, HCLTech, Wipro, and Tech Mahindra together reduced net headcount by 7,389 in FY26. TCS separately reported a net headcount drop of more than 23,000 employees and ended FY26 with 584,519 employees.

This does not mean the India IT market is closed. The broader Indian IT workforce reportedly grew to 59.5 lakh employees in FY26, up from 58.2 lakh. Hiring is shifting away from broad mass intake and toward targeted hiring in AI, cloud, cybersecurity, data, automation testing, production support, internships, apprenticeships, and GCC roles.
