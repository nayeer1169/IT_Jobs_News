## Global IT Job Situation

Global IT hiring is not frozen, but companies are hiring more carefully. The strongest demand is in AI engineering, cloud infrastructure, cybersecurity, data engineering, platform engineering, enterprise software, and AI-related product roles. The weakest areas are routine support, manual QA, internal tooling, non-critical operations, and duplicate middle-management or coordination roles.

The clearest global signal is the layoff count: Layoffs.fyi showed 116,739 tech employees laid off across 164 companies in 2026 as of 2026-06-04. AI is often cited in layoff explanations, but sources such as Axios note that AI is sometimes mixed with broader cost-cutting and market pressure rather than being the only reason.

## India IT Job Situation

India's IT services market is cautious. Moneycontrol reported that the top five Indian IT firms together shrank by 7,389 employees in FY26. This does not mean no one is hiring; it means net headcount growth is weak and companies are replacing broad hiring with targeted hiring.

Freshers should treat the market as competitive but open. Business Standard reported 73% fresher hiring intent for January-June 2026, with Bengaluru at 84% and e-commerce/tech startups at 90%. That supports a practical strategy: apply to big IT companies, but also target startups, GCCs, internships, apprenticeships, QA/testing roles, support engineer roles, and junior developer roles.
