## Quick Summary

The IT hiring landscape for June 2026 shows a sharp divergence. In India, active tech job openings have hit a 28-month low at approximately 93,000, indicating a 14% drop month-on-month and a 17% decline year-on-year. Traditional IT service firms are moving away from volume hiring, prioritizing automation and internal optimization. However, specialized tech talent in AI, machine learning, cloud computing, cybersecurity, and data analytics remains in high demand. Furthermore, Global Capability Centers (GCCs) and emerging tier-2/3 cities like Visakhapatnam, Coimbatore, Jaipur, and Indore are active hot spots for cost-effective talent acquisition.

1. Focus on specialized applications: Tailor your resume specifically for cloud, data engineering, or AI/ML roles rather than general software developer roles.
2. Build and deploy: Maintain 2-3 live projects on GitHub demonstrating practical skills (e.g., an LLM integration pipeline, a secure cloud-deployed backend, or an automated testing suite).
3. Target GCCs and Startups: Explore careers at Global Capability Centers and rising startups as they are actively hiring relative to large-scale traditional IT service firms.
4. Prepare for rigorous screening: Expect aptitude testing, technical assignments, and live DSA/coding rounds, as employers are extremely selective.

## Reliability Notes

- Job listings on official company career portals are more reliable than aggregated third-party job boards.
- Market outlook surveys reflect broad employer sentiment rather than immediate, guaranteed vacancies.
- Rapid shifts in hiring and automation mean job openings can open and close within days.
