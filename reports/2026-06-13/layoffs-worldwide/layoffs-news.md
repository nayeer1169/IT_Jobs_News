## Global Situation

The tech sector is currently in a state of dual-focus: slashing headcount in traditional software maintenance, middle management, and manual operations, while aggressively investing in advanced AI technologies. Companies are shifting their capital expenditure (CapEx) budgets from payroll to high-end compute resources (GPUs) and AI model deployment.

**Roles at higher risk:**
- Non-specialized software maintenance and legacy systems support
- Administrative support and mid-level project coordination
- Manual QA engineering and content moderation
- HR and recruitment specialists in companies freezing general hiring

**Roles less exposed:**
- AI/ML system engineers and neural network researchers
- Cybersecurity and cloud security architects
- DevOps and platform infrastructure engineers
- Enterprise data pipeline and pipeline integration specialists

## India Situation

India's traditional IT export sector is grappling with a shift from resource-based billing to productivity-based delivery. Active tech openings remain at a 28-month low of ~93,000. Large service companies are capping bench sizes and utilizing automated code generation platforms to do the work of entry-level engineers. However, GCCs (Global Capability Centers) continue to expand in major Indian hubs, providing some cushion for mid-to-senior specialized engineers.
