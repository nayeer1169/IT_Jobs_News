## Global Situation

The tech sector remains the primary epicenter of labor adjustments in 2026. According to the latest data from Challenger, Gray & Christmas, tech cuts made up nearly one-third of all U.S. layoffs in the first half of the year. This represents an 83% surge compared to the same period last year. Companies are restructuring operational teams, automation-susceptible administration, and non-core project groups to fund multi-billion dollar capex investments in high-end AI servers and cloud platforms.

**Roles at higher risk:**
- Middle management and non-technical coordinators
- Entry-level code maintenance and manual QA
- Regional administrative support and legacy consulting
- Gaming division back-office and operations

**Roles less exposed:**
- Machine learning research and MLOps practitioners
- Cloud infrastructure architects and SREs
- Cybersecurity specialists and defensive engineers
- Data platform engineers managing large-scale ETL pipelines

## India Situation

Indian technical talent continues to adjust to conservative hiring among major IT services providers. The volatility in offshore operations remains high as companies transition toward flatter domestic models, although GCCs are absorbing a portion of the displaced senior technical talent.
