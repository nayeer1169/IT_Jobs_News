## Global IT Job Situation

Global tech hiring is still under pressure. Layoffs.fyi showed 116,739 tech employees laid off across 164 companies in 2026 when checked on 2026-06-03. The key reasons are:

- AI restructuring: companies are automating routine engineering, support, operations, and back-office work.
- Cost control: companies are protecting margins after slower growth and expensive AI infrastructure spending.
- Role reshaping: junior and routine roles are more exposed, while AI, cloud, cybersecurity, data engineering, and product roles remain comparatively stronger.
- Investor pressure: public tech companies are expected to show productivity gains, so hiring is more selective.

## India IT Job Situation

India is seeing cautious hiring in large IT services, not a broad freeze. Moneycontrol reported that the top five Indian IT firms together reduced headcount by 7,389 in FY26. TCS's reported 12,000-person layoff plan is the biggest negative signal. At the same time, Q4 FY26 was not uniformly bad: TCS, HCLTech, and Wipro added employees sequentially, while Infosys and Tech Mahindra reduced headcount.

For freshers, the market is selective but active. Business Standard reported that 73% of employers surveyed planned to hire freshers in January-June 2026. The strongest signals came from retail, e-commerce/technology startups, manufacturing, and Bengaluru-based IT/startup roles. The important takeaway for freshers: degrees alone are weaker now; projects, internships, GitHub work, certifications, and interview readiness matter more.

## What Freshers Should Do Today

1. Apply first to official portals: TCS NQT/NextStep, Infosys Graduates, Wipro Careers, HCLTech Careers, Accenture Careers, IBM Careers, Cognizant Careers.
2. Build proof-of-work: 2-3 GitHub projects are now more useful than only certificates. Good projects: CRUD app with auth, dashboard using SQL/API, cloud deployment, automation script, testing project.
3. Target skills with demand: Java, Python, SQL, JavaScript/React, cloud basics, Linux, Git, REST APIs, QA automation, data analytics, GenAI tools, cybersecurity fundamentals.
4. Prepare for fresher screening: aptitude, DSA basics, OOP, DBMS, OS/networking basics, resume explanation, and one strong project walkthrough.
5. Do not depend only on mass hiring. Also apply to startups, GCCs, apprenticeships, internships, support engineer roles, QA roles, and junior web developer roles.
