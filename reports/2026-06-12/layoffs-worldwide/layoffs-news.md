## Global Situation

The global tech sector is undergoing a profound transition. Major tech companies are prioritizing capital expenditure (CapEx) for high-cost AI infrastructure like GPUs and data centers. To offset this, they are systematically trimming headcounts, especially in non-core divisions and duplicate engineering groups. 

**Roles at higher risk:**
- Non-specialized software support and operations roles
- Manual testing and QA roles lacking automation skills
- Administrative and project coordination layers
- Legacy product development divisions slated for replacement by AI tools

**Roles less exposed:**
- AI/ML researchers, practitioners, and ML platform engineers
- Cloud architects, DevOps, and Platform engineering teams
- Enterprise data engineering and data pipeline professionals
- Cyber defense and network security staff

## India Situation

India's major IT services companies are dealing with "jobless growth." Combined revenues are increasing, but headcount expansion remains stagnant or negative. This is a direct response to lower bench tolerance and the rollout of automated platforms and AI agents that handle tasks previously assigned to junior engineers. Smaller tech hubs (like Coimbatore and Indore) are seeing some growth as firms relocate roles from Bangalore and Pune to manage costs.

## Reliability Notes

- Layoffs data from public trackers may contain lag or missing private company listings.
- Layoff numbers are estimated and may be subject to revisions by the tracking organizations.
- Workforce headcount reductions include natural attrition and the ending of temporary contracts, which differ from active, targeted layoffs.
