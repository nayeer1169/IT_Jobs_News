## Global Situation

Tech layoffs in 2026 represent a deliberate transition rather than standard cyclical downsizing. The massive CapEx shift toward AI computing and hardware is forcing companies to keep operating budgets extremely tight. 

**Roles at higher risk:**
- Middle management and non-technical coordinators
- Manual QA analysts and junior developers lacking automated testing/AI tooling experience
- Customer support and back-office operators
- Local offshore teams in cases of repatriation (e.g., Opendoor India)

**Roles less exposed:**
- Machine Learning engineers and AI architects
- DevOps, Security, and Cloud platform specialists
- Data engineers handling ETL pipelines and analytics data lakes
- Client-facing technical solution engineers

## India Situation

Indian developers are feeling the effects of both global layoffs and localized structural shifts. In addition to IT services keeping headcounts flat, multiple US firms are centralizing operations (such as Opendoor shutting down its India engineering division), indicating that generic offshore coding tasks are increasingly being automated or consolidated. The job market remains highly competitive, with a focus on cross-skilling into cloud platforms and AI systems to retain employability.
