## Quick Summary

Global tech layoffs remain elevated in 2026. The main reasons are AI-led restructuring, cost control, productivity pressure, slower demand in some software/services segments, and workforce recalibration after earlier over-hiring. India is seeing the same pressure most visibly in large IT services firms, where revenue has grown faster than headcount.

## Global Situation

The global layoff pattern is not simply "AI replaced everyone." The better reading is that companies are using AI and automation to justify leaner operating models while also responding to cost pressure, slower growth, and investor demand for efficiency.

Roles at higher risk:

- routine support and operations
- manual QA without automation skills
- duplicated internal tooling
- back-office and coordination-heavy roles
- non-critical product or project management layers

Roles less exposed:

- AI engineering and applied AI product work
- cloud infrastructure and platform engineering
- cybersecurity
- data engineering
- reliability, production support, and customer-facing technical roles

## India Situation

India's IT services sector is showing jobless growth. Economic Times reported that active technology job openings were about 93,000 in June 2026, down from 112,000 a year earlier. It also reported that six large IT firms grew revenue from $95.9 billion in FY23 to $103.1 billion in FY26 while combined headcount stayed around 1.9 million.

The practical meaning: Indian IT companies are trying to generate more revenue per employee. Automation, AI tools, lower bench tolerance, and selective hiring are replacing earlier mass hiring patterns.
