## Global Situation

The global tech sector is undergoing structural adjustments centered around the "AI Factor," which is cited in approximately 55% of all layoff events this year. While companies continue to reduce headcount in traditional roles like legacy software engineering and manual operations, they are aggressively reallocating capital budgets toward AI infrastructure and computing hardware. However, analysts note that many firms executing these AI-driven cuts have yet to see clear gains in operating margins.

**Roles at higher risk:**
- Legacy software developers and manual quality assurance analysts
- Customer support specialists and back-office administrative staff
- Mid-level project managers and generalist engineers
- Content coordinators and database administrators

**Roles less exposed:**
- Machine Learning (ML) engineers and AI model developers
- Cloud security engineers and platform architects
- Advanced data pipeline (ETL/data lake) engineers
- Solution architects with direct client-facing responsibilities

## India Situation

The hiring market for Indian IT professionals remains highly competitive as local talent pools are swelled by thousands of tech professionals returning from or impacted by layoffs at global tech companies. Cautious spending by clients has forced domestic IT services firms to prioritize resource efficiency over headcount expansion. Reskilling in specialized, AI-augmented engineering tracks is becoming the primary path for maintaining employability in India's major tech hubs.
