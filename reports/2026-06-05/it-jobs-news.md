## Global IT Job Situation

Global tech hiring is selective rather than broadly strong. The safest demand areas remain AI engineering, data infrastructure, cloud, cybersecurity, platform engineering, enterprise software, and roles that improve revenue or automation. The weakest areas are routine support, manual QA, duplicated internal tooling, back-office operations, and roles that can be automated or consolidated.

There are two important layoff signals today:

- Layoffs.fyi shows 116,739 publicly tracked tech layoffs across 164 companies in 2026.
- Tom's Hardware reported 38,242 US tech-sector announced cuts in May 2026 and 123,653 announced cuts for the sector in 2026, citing Challenger-style layoff data.

These numbers are not identical because they track different scopes and methodologies. The practical conclusion is still the same: tech companies are continuing to cut or restructure while hiring narrowly in high-priority areas.

## India IT Job Situation

India's IT services hiring remains muted at the largest firms. Moneycontrol reported that TCS, Infosys, HCLTech, Wipro, and Tech Mahindra together reduced net headcount by 7,389 in FY26. TCS separately reported a net headcount drop of more than 23,000 employees, closing FY26 with 584,519 employees.

At the same time, the broader Indian IT workforce reportedly grew to 59.5 lakh employees in FY26, up from 58.2 lakh. This means demand has not disappeared; it is shifting toward selected skills, GCC roles, startups, AI/cloud/data work, and entry-level roles where candidates can show job-ready ability.
