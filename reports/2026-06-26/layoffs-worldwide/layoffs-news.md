## Global Situation

Workforce realignments in late June 2026 reflect a dual trend of structural contraction and specialized tech investment. While general layoffs remain high (averaging over 1,000 cuts daily), some executives are questioning the long-term effectiveness of mass headcount cuts. There is an increasing focus on targeted re-skilling rather than sweeping redundancies to ensure companies retain specialized domain knowledge while executing AI shifts.

**Roles at higher risk:**
- Non-AI-integrated developer roles
- Manual testing and QA roles
- Game development, design, and content QA roles
- Middle management and operations coordination

**Roles less exposed:**
- Machine Learning (MLOps) and deep learning engineers
- Cloud infrastructure architects and SREs
- Cybersecurity specialists and network threat analysts
- Government and defense technology developers

## India Situation

The hiring freeze among major Indian IT service providers has resulted in a multi-year low in entry-level openings. Developers are shifting their focus to Global Capability Centers (GCCs), which are offering stable, high-value product engineering roles, particularly in time-zone-aligned remote structures.
